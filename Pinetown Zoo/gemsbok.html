GEMSBOKS

Gemsboks are large antelopes with long, straight horns and distinctive black and white markings. They are well adapted to arid environments and can be found in the Kalahari Desert and other parts of southern Africa. Gemsboks are known for their incredible stamina and can run at high speeds over long distances.

All gemsoks have several distinctive features, the most notable of which is a long neck and slender legs. Their coat is a striking combination of tan and white, with bold black markings on the face and legs. Gemsoks are well adapted to their environment, with a unique ability to regulate their body temperature and conserve water.

Gemsboks are herbivorous and can be found in different habitats including savannahs, forests, deserts and marshes. They prefer to stay near water.
